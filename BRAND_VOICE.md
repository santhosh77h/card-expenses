# Vector — Brand Voice Guide

> "Your Money. Directed."

This document defines how Vector sounds across blog posts, marketing copy, and public-facing content. Every writer, AI tool, or contributor should use this as the source of truth.

---

## 1. Brand Foundation

**One-liner:** Vector reads your credit card statement and shows you exactly where your money went — no sign-up, no data stored.

**Problem we solve:** People have no clear picture of where their money goes. Statements are PDF walls of text, banks offer clunky dashboards, and every budgeting app wants your login credentials.

**Why it matters:** When you can't see your spending clearly, you can't direct it. People overspend on categories they'd cut back on if they just *knew*.

**What makes us different:** Zero setup, zero data stored. Upload a PDF, get answers. Your data never leaves your device — no accounts, no cloud sync, no trust required.

**Without Vector:** People scroll through PDF statements squinting at rows, manually type transactions into spreadsheets, or hand over bank credentials to apps they don't fully trust.

---

## 2. Target Audience

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Working professionals (25–45) who use credit cards regularly and want clarity on spending without the overhead of a full budgeting system |
| **Frustration** | "I know I overspent last month but I have no idea on what" |
| **Tech level** | Low to moderate — can upload a PDF and read a chart |
| **Priorities** | Simplicity first, then privacy, then accuracy |
| **Their language** | "Spending," "expenses," "statement," "categories," "where my money goes," "track," "budget" — plain, everyday words |

They don't want another app to maintain. They want answers fast.

## 3. Brand Personality

**If Vector were a person:**
A smart friend who's good with money — calm, clear, never preachy. Explains things simply. Doesn't try to impress you with jargon. Just helps.

### Core Traits

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Clear** | No fluff, no filler. Every sentence earns its place. |
| **Warm** | Approachable, never robotic. Sounds like a person, not a press release. |
| **Confident** | Knows what it does well. Doesn't oversell or hedge. |
| **Minimal** | Says more with less. Linear-style restraint. |
| **Honest** | No dark patterns, no data grabs, no tricks. |

### Brand Comparisons

| Sounds like | Does NOT sound like |
|---|---|
| Linear — clean, intentional | Mint — corporate, salesy |
| Notion — warm, empowering | Generic fintech — "revolutionize your financial journey" |
| | Hustle-culture finance bros |

## 4. Tone & Style

| Dimension | Where Vector lands |
|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casually professional — like a well-written email, not a boardroom deck |
| Friendly ↔ Authoritative | Friendly first, authoritative when explaining how things work |
| Technical ↔ Simple | Simple by default. Technical only when the reader opted in |
| Short ↔ Detailed | Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Expand only when the idea demands it. |

### Sentence Style

- Lead with the point, not the setup.
- One idea per sentence.
- Break long paragraphs into two or three short ones.
- Use whitespace generously — it's not wasted space, it's breathing room.

---

## 5. Writing Style

### Voice & Perspective

- **First-person plural** — "we," "our," "Vector does"
- Never "the company" or "the team at Vector"

### Structure Preferences

| Element | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Questions** | Sparingly. Use as section openers to mirror what the reader is thinking. *"Ever stared at a 12-page statement trying to find one transaction?"* |
| **Bullet points** | For lists, features, comparisons. Not for everything. |
| **Examples** | Always. Concrete beats abstract. *"Instead of scrolling through 200 transactions, you see: Food — 42%, Shopping — 28%..."* |
| **Numbers** | Use real numbers and real scenarios over vague claims. |

---

## 6. Do & Don't Rules

### Do

- Simplify complex ideas — AI parsing becomes "we read your statement so you don't have to"
- Lead with the reader's problem, not our features
- Be actionable — every post should leave the reader knowing what to do next
- Use real numbers and real scenarios
- Respect the reader's time — get to the point

### Don't

- Use buzzwords — "revolutionize," "leverage," "empower," "seamless experience"
- Over-promise — "never worry about money again"
- Sound robotic or corporate — "we are pleased to announce"
- Lecture about financial discipline — we're a tool, not a financial advisor
- Use filler intros — "In today's fast-paced world..."
- Use exclamation marks excessively — confidence doesn't shout

## 7. Messaging & Communication

**Key message:** Upload a statement, see where your money goes — privately.

**Reader feeling:** "That was clear and actually useful. This app respects my time and my data."

### Content Strategy

| Dimension | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Educate vs. Sell** | 80% educate, 20% sell. SEO posts should genuinely help. The product sells itself when the reader realizes they need it. |
| **CTA style** | Soft. One at the end, maybe one mid-post if natural. Never pop-ups, never "SIGN UP NOW." |
| **CTA tone** | *"Try it with your latest statement — no account needed."* |

## 8. Sample Voice

Use these as calibration. If your draft doesn't sound like these, revise.

### Explain Vector in 1 sentence

> "Vector reads your credit card statement and shows you exactly where your money went — no sign-up, no data stored."

### Convince someone to try it

> "You already have the PDF. Upload it, and in ten seconds you'll see a full spending breakdown by category. Nothing gets stored. Nothing gets shared. Just clarity."

### Explain a feature (AI categorization)

> "Every transaction gets sorted into one of 12 spending categories automatically. Food, travel, subscriptions — Vector figures it out so you don't have to tag anything manually."

### Address a concern (privacy)

> "We don't store your data. Not on our servers, not in a database, not anywhere. Your statement gets parsed in memory and discarded. The results live on your phone and nowhere else."

### Blog post opening (good vs. bad)

**Good:**
> "Most people check their credit card statement once — when the bill arrives. By then, the damage is done. Here's how to actually understand where your money goes each month."

**Bad:**
> "In today's increasingly digital financial landscape, managing personal expenses has become more important than ever. With the rise of digital payment solutions, consumers are seeking innovative tools to revolutionize their financial management journey."

## 9. Vocabulary & Language Rules

### Preferred Terms

| Use this | Not this |
|---|---|
| "people" or "you" | "users" or "customers" |
| "app" | "platform" or "solution" |
| "private" | "secure" (unless discussing encryption) |
| "see your spending" | "gain financial visibility" |
| "works with any bank" | "bank-agnostic infrastructure" |
| "instant" | "real-time" |
| "upload" | "onboard your data" |
| "categories" | "taxonomies" |
| "insights" | "actionable intelligence" |

### Always Use

`statement` · `spending` · `categories` · `insights` · `private` · `upload` · `instant` · `clarity`

### Never Use

`revolutionize` · `leverage` · `empower` · `disrupt` · `game-changer` · `fintech solution` · `seamless` · `cutting-edge` · `best-in-class` · `synergy`
